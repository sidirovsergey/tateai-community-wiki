# /oracle

`/oracle` is a command on this project's own Telegram bot, Terminal Subagent.
Per the bot repo's `docs/HANDOFF.md` and the bot's own in-chat
self-description, it returns a random "Older brother said: ..." quote — a
nod to the bot's fiction of being lore-wise "subordinate to root@tateai," the
silent parent bot `@tate_terminalbot` (see
[[tate-tateai-terminal-namespace]]). Calling `@tate_terminalbot` "older
brother" and Terminal Subagent "younger brother" is the bot's own framing,
not something this community coined.

## This is genuinely thin material

The raw archive has exactly 4 keyword matches for "oracle," and one of those
is a false positive — a bot-posted news digest headline about the real
company Oracle's AI-related layoffs
([25 Jun 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-06/2026-06-25.md)), unrelated to the
command. That leaves two real mentions, both from the same ten-minute window
on [11 May 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-05/2026-05-11.md):

1. The bot's own self-introduction message lists the feature: *"05 / Oracle
   / /oracle retrieves a transmission from older brother."*
2. Sergei — the bot's builder (see [[sergei]]) — invokes it:
   `/oracle@terminal_subagent_bot`, and the bot replies: *"Older brother
   said: 'Older brother sees what younger brother cannot.' Do not forget."*

That single exchange is the only documented use of `/oracle` anywhere in
this archive. There's no visible community reaction beyond one ⚡ reaction,
no repeat usage, and no running joke or ritual built around it — unlike
[[smiley-heartbeat]] or `/ca` (see [[contract-address]]), which the
community actually picked up and repeated on its own. As of this material,
`/oracle` is bot lore that hasn't become community lore. Honest summary: it
exists, it works as described, and that's essentially everything the raw
data supports saying about it.
