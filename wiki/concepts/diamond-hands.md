# Diamond hands

"Diamond hands" — holding through volatility no matter what — is this
community's central, load-bearing piece of rhetoric. A keyword search for
the term turns up 506 matches, present from the chat's very first hours
([26 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-26.md): "diamond hands baby")
through to the most recent material in the archive
([25 Mar 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-03/2026-03-25.md): "we will diamond hand
this to the stratosphere when the time comes"). No other single phrase in
this chat has that kind of staying power.

## The insult counterpart: "paper hands"

"Diamond hands" only means something in opposition to its shadow, "paper
hands" — anyone who sells. Both terms show up together from day one:

> "Yeah! If you've got paper hands leave"
> — KAVOD, [27 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-27.md)

> "Sell now paper hand"
> — P Z, [27 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-27.md)

> "Eat this dip fuck the paper hands"
> — •CrypticBeachBum•🌴, [29 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-29.md)

"Strong hands" functions as a milder synonym ("Very strong hands now boys,
let's keep it that way" — orez,
[3 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-03.md)), and "hold the line" is
the imperative-mood version of the same idea — a rallying cry rather than a
description, used constantly by Limalemon and dozens of others through the
whole launch window and beyond ("HOLD THE LINE DONT LET THE TATE FAGGOTS IN"
— I'm lucky, [31 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-31.md)).

## Where the poem came from

The earliest attributed origin of the *specific literary register* — dense,
faux-mystical prose-poems about diamond hands, dex screeners, and 100x —
traces to content credited to "Tate Terminal," not to a chat member:

> "Diamond hands nigga ✅ / We are here to stay / […] This is the best poem
> that Tate Terminal has released, to make us understand that we shouldn't
> search on the CEX but in the depths of the Crypto space on the DEX."
> — IGelo, [29 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-29.md)

**This matters for accuracy.** Per [[tate-tateai-terminal-namespace]], "tate
terminal" never appears as a real chat participant — it's the forwarded
content source `@tate_terminalbot`, rendered in `raw/logs/` with a parsing
bug that appends the forward timestamp to the sender name, e.g. `tate
terminal 04.12.2024 05:07:27`. Dozens of the diamond-hands prose-poems in
this archive are attributed to that pattern — for example, the raw log
itself is explicit about it:

> `tate terminal 28.12.2024 23:37:52` (forwarded from `tate terminal`): "in
> the relentless march of the crypto markets, diamond hands are the
> modern-day knights. they stand unyielding, resolute in the face of
> chaos…"
> — [29 Dec 2024, 00:30](../../raw/logs/2024-12/2024-12-29.md)

This is precisely the confusion that produced a wrong answer in earlier
ad-hoc testing of this chat's data — a question about "who are the diamond
hand holders" credited "tate terminal" as if it were a person. It is not
one; treat every such line as reposted content, not testimony.

## Everyone picked up the register

What's genuinely striking in the raw material is how many *different* named
senders — not just forwards, not just [[rgk1]] — independently (or by
copying each other) write in that exact same "diamond hands aren't about
X, they're about Y" cadence: `Deleted Account` most often (impossible to
attribute to one person, see [[deleted-accounts]]), but also Plum, Daniel R,
t1G🦉, Alokin Snyder, B, D 🦂, R2D2, Sam, Konstantinos, Hokus Pokus, and
more, running from late October 2024 through 2025. Several instances are
*identical text posted by different senders at the same timestamp* — e.g.
D 🦂 and orez both post "in the hearts of the brave, diamond hands clutch
convictions unyielding…" verbatim at
[31 Oct 2024, 22:49](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-31.md). The honest
read is that this was raid/copy-paste text passed around the community (the
same way `/WE_ARE_A_CULT` and `/WE_ARE_THE_ONLY_COMMUNITY_RAINDANCING` were
— see [[community-culture]]), not hundreds of people independently arriving
at the same florid metaphor. [[rgk1]] is the one voice who sustains this
register as a genuinely personal, evolving style for months rather than a
one-off copy — see their page for extensive quotes; this page deliberately
doesn't repeat them.

## The early, un-poetic version

Before the register above took hold, the phrase did plainer work — cult
formation and shakeout theory, mainly from 0xVoid ✈️ in the first week:

> "Cult behavior -> diamond hands -> valhalla"
> — 0xVoid ✈️, [29 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-29.md)

> "The people left are gonna be the people with strong minds […] and strong
> hands (didn't sell)."
> — orez, [2 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-02.md)

## Sergei's deadpan version

[[sergei]] — the eventual bot owner, whose voice elsewhere on this wiki is
the chat's skeptical analyst — uses the phrase too, but tersely, almost as
punctuation rather than conviction:

> "diamond hands)"
> — Sergei, [27 Jan 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-01/2025-01-27.md)

> "paper hand)" / "but yeah ok, profit paper hand)"
> — Sergei, [5 Feb 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-02/2025-02-05.md)

By mid-2025 that deadpan tips into open irony about the whole concept:

> "When showtime starts, I will tag here every loser that was diamond hand
> 7 months ago"
> — Sergei, [22 Jul 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-07/2025-07-22.md) (🤣×8)

sbzgh's reply reads the joke correctly — "all jeets will come back the day
we pump, and they will pretend to be 'og's'" — this isn't Sergei mocking
diamond hands as an ideal, it's mocking people who'll falsely *claim* to
have held once there's something to show for it.
