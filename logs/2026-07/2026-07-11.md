**01:13** `Sergei`: Kazakhstan Moves to Exempt Crypto Investors from Income Tax

On July 7, 2026, President Kassym-Jomart Tokayev signed a decree "On Measures to Stimulate and Develop the Digital Assets Industry in the Republic of Kazakhstan," and the Ministry of Artificial Intelligence and Digital Development clarified the details the following day.

The gist: Kazakhstan plans to exempt individuals' income from crypto transactions from personal income tax (IPN) — but only for operations conducted through licensed, regulated Kazakhstani platforms (not foreign exchanges).

The strategy: rather than restricting crypto activity, the government wants to incentivize Kazakhstanis to voluntarily move their digital assets from unregulated foreign wallets/exchanges to domestic licensed providers. Financial analyst Arman Beisembayev summed it up as "carrot, not stick" — offering tax breaks instead of tighter controls to pull capital back into a regulated environment.

Also included in the decree:
- Development of cross-border settlement mechanisms using digital assets and stablecoins, opening new channels for import/export businesses
- Continued expansion of tokenized financial instruments and national digital finance infrastructure

**01:16** `Sergei`: Kazakhstan Launches eResidency Program

Kazakhstan now offers eResidency — a digital ID letting foreign nationals remotely access the country's financial and business services, no travel required. Operated by Verum Payments Limited under the Astana International Financial Centre (AIFC), regulated by AFSA.

What you get ($499/year):
- Digital ID Card + Individual Identification Number (IIN)
- Remote bank account opening (Freedom Bank, RBK Bank; Bereke Bank joining soon)
- Access to investment services via licensed brokers
- eSIM with a Kazakhstan number
- Online company registration in the AIFC

Good to know:
- Doesn't grant Kazakhstan tax residency or a visa/residency permit — Kazakhstan participates in OECD's CRS, so your financial data still gets shared with your actual home tax authority
- Payment accepted via card or regulated stablecoins (USDT/USDC)
- Full KYC/AML and sanctions screening required (UN, EU, OFAC, UK lists) — Russian citizens can apply but face Enhanced Due Diligence
- Process takes 1–3 business days, status valid 12 months

Useful for entrepreneurs, freelancers, digital nomads, and investors wanting a foothold in Kazakhstan's digital economy. (reactions: ❤x3)
