# Ben

A launch-rush teenager who never really left. Ben shows up two days after the
chat's founding, posts in dense, sleepless bursts through the entire
Oct–Nov 2024 hype cycle, and then keeps checking back in — at dwindling but
real frequency — for the next year and a half, all the way to the edge of
this corpus (Apr 2026).

## The launch-era voice: broke, believing, 18

Ben's earliest messages establish him immediately as a true believer with
real skin in the game, not an observer. He names his age and situation
outright:

> "I'm 18 man" / "Work in a restaurant" / "1.8-2k monthly so not terrible for
> my age"
> — [5 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-05.md)

The money anxiety is constant and played for both genuine stress and bit —
he repeatedly threatens to raid his own savings or his parents' accounts to
buy more:

> "If this doesn't reach 300 mil mc im gonna take 2 mil from the bank and
> boost this fucker" ... "But take I mean steal"
> — [4 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-04.md)

> "Just stole from my dads pension to add more"
> — [3 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-03.md)

By January 2025 he's doing the same date-numerology theorizing that defines
the chat's secondary hype wave (see [[launch-and-origins]]), laying out a
whole evidence list for why a specific Saturday was "the day":

> "So far, I think we can all agree this Saturday is the day. / 1. Flux said
> referenced 79 days... / 2. The terminal has mentioned Saturday in a few
> tweets... / 3. @Mangobananavelimon used chat gbt to break down some of what
> the terminal bot was referring in the tweets... / BUY MORE :)"
> — [9 Jan 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-01/2025-01-09.md) (🔥×4, 👍×2)

## Later years: two running bits, one shill attempt

Ben's message rate drops hard after early 2025 (consistent with the
community-wide falloff, see [[launch-and-origins]]) but never goes fully
silent. Two catchphrases recur across widely spaced dates, each landing
consistent reactions — evidence he's still recognized as a fixture rather
than posting into a void:

- **"72 hours"** — a bare two-word post, six times between
  [9 Jun](../../raw/logs/2025-06/2025-06-09.md) and [6 Jul
  2025](../../raw/logs/2025-07/2025-07-06.md), always drawing 🔥 reactions.
- **"It's happening [guys/soon/today]"** — the same holding-pattern hype
  phrase repeated through March–April 2026, e.g. *"It's happening guys"* [9
  Mar 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-03/2026-03-09.md), *"Today is the day fellow
  Tate aiers"* [10 Apr 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-04/2026-04-10.md).

In June 2025 he also tried, briefly and semi-apologetically, to shill an
unrelated play to the group by DM:

> "I hold Tate ai, read the text I mean no bad intention just passing on some
> alpha to you chads... Don't report read. Fade it if you want"
> — [26 Jun 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-06/2025-06-26.md)

## The April 2026 admin incident

By 2026 Ben had been made a group admin. On [19 Apr
2026](../../raw/logs/2026-04/2026-04-19.md), per [[sbzgh]]'s account (Ben's
own log has no messages covering the incident itself), Ben went on a banning
spree that caught [[fluxcapacitor]] along with ordinary members:

> "ben the faggot banned everyone" ... "he banned flux too" ... "we can't
> [unban them], he is an admin" ... "i sent him a dm to ask if he can remove
> ben from admins"
> — sbzgh, [19 Apr 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-04/2026-04-19.md)

sbzgh spent the following day unbanning the affected members and personally
inviting FluxCapacitor back to the chat
([20 Apr 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-04/2026-04-20.md)). Ben's own raw log
doesn't include commentary on the episode — it simply stops the evening
before it happened and picks back up days later with no reference to it.
Nothing here says why it happened; it's recorded as what the other side of
the chat saw and had to clean up.
