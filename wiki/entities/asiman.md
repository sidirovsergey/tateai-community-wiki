# Asiman

One of the founding week's most intense on-chain investigators — present
from the chat's literal first minutes and gone entirely by [9 Nov
2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-09.md), a compressed two-week presence
that packs 1,223 messages into what amounts to the "ticker hunt" documented
on [[launch-and-origins]] (Asiman is already quoted there as the source for
the "72 hours" game-clock theory and the earliest wallet-tracing thread).
This page adds the fuller picture: the emotional arc underneath the
investigation, and a member who was — by his own, uncorroborated account —
briefly an admin before Limalemon.

## The opening line

Asiman's first logged message is also the earliest sampled quote used to
kick off this wiki's own quality checks, and it holds up: a reply, four
minutes into the exported chat's life, already mid-conversation about
whether `$TateAI` is the real ticker:

> "If it is the right one (it obviously seems) we retire"
> — [26 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-26.md) (❤×2)

## The on-chain investigator

Within the first hour Asiman is walking the room through a live wallet
trace — deployer funding, a Coinbase refund, a transfer to a wallet he
attributes to "Tate Terminal" — the exact material [[launch-and-origins]]
and [[contract-address]] cite as the week's founding evidence:

> "Dev wallet refunded by Coinbase and used 9 sol to create $TateAi... And
> send tokens to Tate Terminal wallet, and didn't do anything since that
> time"
> — [26 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-26.md)

> "I think he knows we are tracking the wallet, so created other tokens to
> confuse us"
> — [26 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-26.md)

He treats every price move in the first 24 hours as Andrew Tate personally
manipulating the chart in real time — "Tate is controlling price," "He is
playing with us" — the paranoid-excitement register [[community-culture]]
identifies as the week's dominant mood, and by the second day is explicitly
framing the whole exercise as more valuable than the money at stake:

> "This W guy has been more helpful than 90% of people who is here. We are
> just speculating and investigating. It is the thing that will remain at
> the end if we wrong. Maybe we can lose money, but we made connections and
> we had fun while investigating"
> — [27 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-27.md) (👏×7)

## Self-declared admin, before Limalemon

Three days before [[limalemon]]'s well-documented admin promotion, Asiman
is already fielding unban/ban requests and issuing `/unban` and `/report`
commands as if he had moderator standing, and later states outright that he
once held the role:

> "I was the first member and, i was admin, but don't know why u took my
> role man"
> — [30 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-30.md)

This is Asiman's own claim, uncorroborated elsewhere in the sampled
material — worth recording as a data point about the founding week's
informal, contested leadership rather than as settled fact.

## Turning on $Tate

By 28 Oct, Asiman's conviction has hardened into open hostility toward the
rival "cabal" coin, `$Tate` — checking top-holder wallets, declaring them
bots, and getting banned from the rival group for it:

> "I fuck $Tate, ut is obviously scam, don't be fooled / $Tateai is the one"
> — [28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md) (🔥×3)

> "I just got banned from $Tate group, i proud"
> — [30 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-30.md)

## Code-switching at the DEX listing

At the moment `$TateAI` lists on Lavarage — one of the week's clearest highs
— Asiman briefly drops out of English into Turkish/Azerbaijani with another
member, unprompted:

> "Dex'de listelendi, bot da Dex baresinde danisir, Mexc CEX'di" ("It got
> listed on DEX, the bot only talks about DEX listings, MEXC was the CEX")
> / "Bizim token dogru olandi" ("Our token was the right one")
> — [28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md)

## Honest flag

Asiman also repeatedly posts slur-laden slash-chants (`/Faggot_`,
`/WE_WILL_WIN_NIGGERS`) dozens of times in a row during hype spikes — the
same raid/chant format [[community-culture]] documents chat-wide, not a
distinguishing trait of his, but worth noting plainly rather than
smoothing over.
