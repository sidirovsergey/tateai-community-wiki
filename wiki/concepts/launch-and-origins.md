# Launch and origins — the ticker hunt

This page covers the *content* of the chat's founding week
(26 Oct – 2 Nov 2024) — what people actually said and did — as a companion
to [[timeline]], which already covers that period's message-volume arc in
detail. See [[contract-address]] for the specific, drawn-out saga of
whether/when the contract address itself would be confirmed.

## A supergroup born mid-argument

The export begins with a service message, not a greeting:

> `[SERVICE] Terminal of Tate $TateAI converted a basic group to this
> supergroup «Terminal of Tate $TateAI»`
> — [26 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-26.md)

The very next messages are not introductions — they're people already
mid-speculation, picking up a conversation that predates the supergroup
conversion itself: *"yo" / "u think its tateai>" / "i got in at 45k avg
price when coin was released" / "I am 99% sure this is ticker."* Within the
first four minutes multiple people are already asking whether the group
"surpasses $daddy" (Andrew Tate's other, already-established coin). There is
no clean founding moment to point to — the group's entire premise, from
message one, is already the ticker hunt.

## The premise: a game with a 72-hour clock

The community's working theory, repeated constantly through the week, was
that Andrew Tate had deliberately hidden a "real" token behind clues —
tweets, livestream ("Emergency Meeting") references, wallet activity,
imagery — and that finding it was a designed game, not luck:

> "Yesterday morning he said the game will begin in 72 hours"
> — Asiman, [26 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-26.md)

This "he's testing us" framing is what makes the week read as detective
work rather than ordinary memecoin hype — members treat wallet transactions
and tweet timing as evidence to be gathered and cross-examined, not just
price action to react to.

## The caller: WWW | Alpha Tracker

The chat credits one specific member with calling `$TateAI` before the
group even existed in its current form:

> "@WWWatcher u called it from 45k and it went to 5m / You might be the
> earliest caller for this shit if it runs to millions"
> — Deleted Account, [26 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-26.md)
>
> "I was the first caller ya"
> — WWW | Alpha Tracker, same thread

WWW | Alpha Tracker turns out to have round-tripped the early gains rather
than holding (*"i think i sold around 1.2m but i only had put in .5 sol"*)
and stays a minor, occasional presence through the week (moderator-adjacent
by [27 Oct](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-27.md): *"Who made you mod
again / Love it"*) rather than becoming a major recurring character — but
the "earliest caller" framing sticks and is referenced repeatedly as a
credibility marker for the ticker.

## The method: on-chain sleuthing plus Easter-egg reading

Two parallel investigative styles run through the week, often from the same
people in the same breath.

**Wallet tracing.** Within the first hour, Asiman walks the chat through a
live on-chain investigation of the token's deployer wallet — that it was
"refunded by Coinbase," used 9 SOL to create the token, and sent tokens to a
wallet attributed to "Tate Terminal":

> "Dev wallet refunded by Coinbase and used 9 sol to create $TateAi... And
> send tokens to Tate Terminal wallet, and didn't do anything since that
> time"
> — Asiman, [26 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-26.md)

Others post Solscan transaction links as evidence in the same style
throughout the week (CattSoup: *"Evidence of token creation"*, with a raw
Solscan link, same day).

**Easter-egg reading.** In parallel, members treat Tate's public
output — tweets, banner images, streamed imagery — as a cipher. A cobra/Black
Mamba symbol in the token's artwork gets read as a Kobe Bryant reference
tying back to Tate ("E: That's a cobra symbol... Think Kobe and Black Mamba
symbol"); a "Matrix Easter egg" in a background image gets flagged
([28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md)); and the contract
address's leading letters (see [[contract-address]]) get read as a
Terminator reference within the first few hours. By
[28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md), members are explicitly
comparing the *density* of clues between candidates as evidence in itself:

> "Compare all the cool Easter eggs we found for this coin compared to the
> cabal one…. There are literally so many, if it's not this whoever made it
> is a genius who planned it for months."
> — P, [28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md)

> "If it's $TATEAI, Tate can brag for hours about how cool he made it all,
> with Easter eggs and how he fooled everyone... But if it's $TATE then it
> was just obvious and boring and his whole mysterious aura is fucked."
> — Artur, [28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md)

Not everyone bought into the pattern-matching. [[sergei]], who arrives in
the chat on [28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md), is the
week's consistent skeptical voice, pushing back on the same day: *"you dive
to deep... this images is just your imagination... don't get them all as
ticker signs... just pure math"* — see his own page for the fuller record of
that role.

## The candidates

Several tickers were in active contention, not just two. Early in the first
hour, W is already second-guessing between `$topg` and `$tateai` ("both my
guess about $topg and my second guess on $tateai seem off here branding
wise"), Centurion mentions "Nigterminal" as another option worth discussing,
and by the next day the field has narrowed publicly to a short list:

> "Tate(cabal), TateAI, buttcoin"
> — 0xVoid ✈️, [27 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-27.md)

`$TATE` — nicknamed "the cabal coin" or just "cabal" throughout this
material — is treated as the chief rival candidate all week, generally
characterized as the *less* interesting, more "obvious" answer precisely
because it lacked the density of clues members had found for `$TateAI`
(Hecker, [27 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-27.md): *"16 mil one
is lead by cabals and they updated all stuff"*). `$RNT` and `$DADDY`
(Tate's own, already-established coin) round out the small set of named
rivals referenced elsewhere in the archive — see
[[tate-tateai-terminal-namespace]] for how `$RNT` in particular later spun
off its own unrelated shill-bot presence in this very chat.

## The week's emotional arc

The tone moves in a fairly clean arc across the seven days:

- **26–27 Oct** — frantic, paranoid excitement. Every price move is read as
  Tate personally manipulating the chart: *"He is playing with us"*, *"Tate
  is controlling price."*
- **28–30 Oct** — peak confidence, driven by the Easter-egg comparisons
  above and a widely-shared belief the "AI campus" launch (part of Tate's
  Real World platform, expected around 1 Nov) would force a reveal. Sam Ho,
  [30 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-30.md): *"CONFIRMED THE
  TERMINAL IS READING OUR ACCOUNT. TICKER $TATEAI GETTING ANNOUNCED ANY
  MINUTE NOWWWW."*
- **31 Oct – 2 Nov** — the campus launch passes with no reveal (see
  [[contract-address]] for exactly what happened instead: the terminal's
  bio briefly showed the *rival* coin's address). The mood shifts from
  expecting confirmation to deciding not to need it. The clearest statement
  of that shift is a long post several members independently repost
  verbatim on [3 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-03.md):

  > "Does it matter? They have nothing... We have all the theories, all the
  > lore... We are the only Tate coin despite not being mentioned and
  > endorsed by Tate that's still here every day. / What happens next may
  > will surprise everybody, but us. / We won't be surprised."

## No clean ending

Unlike a typical scavenger hunt, this one doesn't resolve with an answer
being revealed — it resolves with the community deciding the hunt was over
because *it* had decided, not because Tate or the terminal ever confirmed
anything unambiguous. That's the throughline into everything that follows:
the CA-drop saga in [[contract-address]] runs for weeks past this window
with the same pattern, and per [[timeline]], most of the crowd that arrived
for this specific mystery had left within six weeks once no clean
confirmation ever came.
