# sbzgh

The clearest embodiment of what [[overview|the overview page]] calls the
"small, steady core" that kept this chat alive after the launch rush burned
out. sbzgh is a late arrival by this chat's standards — first message
[27 Jun 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-06/2025-06-27.md), eight months after
founding — but from that point on is one of the most consistent presences in
the archive, active as both a moderator and the group's warmest, most
reliable "good morning" voice through to the end of the corpus in July 2026.

## The moderator

sbzgh holds and uses moderation tools throughout his entire run — `/ban`,
`/unban`, `/unmute`, `/filter` commands appear dozens of times from his first
week ([29 Jun 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-06/2025-06-29.md): `/filter
evidence2`) through his last logged month ([14 Jul
2026](../../raw/logs/2026-07/2026-07-14.md): `/ban`). He's matter-of-fact
about the volume of it: *"lmao 813 people banned on this group"*
([2 Oct 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-10/2025-10-02.md)). His most notable
moderation moment isn't a ban he made but one he had to undo — see
[[ben]] for the April 2026 incident where Ben, then a fellow admin, banned a
swath of members including [[fluxcapacitor]]. sbzgh's raw log captures the
cleanup in real time:

> "ben the faggot banned everyone" ... "we can't [unban them], he is an
> admin" ... "i sent him a dm to ask if he can remove ben from admins"
> — [19 Apr 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-04/2026-04-19.md)

> "i unbanned him and sent an invite" ... "try to dm him the link guys, cuz
> if im alone maybe he will think im trying to scam him lmao"
> — [20 Apr 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-04/2026-04-20.md), re-inviting
> FluxCapacitor back into the chat.

## "gm bros :)" — the heartbeat within the heartbeat

Bare `:)` and `gm :)` posts are sbzgh's single most common message shape (see
[[smiley-heartbeat]] for the chat-wide pattern), but his version has a
distinct, warm, brotherhood-flavored voice — "gm bros", "gm fam", "gm
brothers" — and the reaction counts on these grow visibly over his run, from
single digits in mid-2025 to consistently 6–10+ 🔥/❤ by mid-2026, e.g. *"GM
:)"* drawing ❤×11 on [13 Oct 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-10/2025-10-13.md).
That trajectory reads as a community that came to specifically look forward
to his check-ins, not just tolerate ambient `:)` noise.
