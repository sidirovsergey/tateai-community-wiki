# Wilson

A pure launch-week figure — one of the most prolific on-chain sleuths in
the chat's founding days, then gone. First message
[28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md), sampled material ends
[9 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-09.md), and Wilson never
appears again anywhere in the material sampled for this page — a run of
almost exactly twelve days, all of it inside the chat's opening "which
ticker is real" scramble (see [[launch-and-origins]]).

## Voice: solscan detective, all-in believer

Wilson's messages are dense with wallet-tracing detail — creator wallets,
bonding-curve mechanics, which top holders sold and when — delivered in the
same breathless run-on style as the rest of the launch-week crowd, but with
more actual transaction data than most:

> "Ok my last message, I've been checking out all the pumpfun buyers of
> tateai and they've been buying clips then selling 10% instantly, as if it
> was a bot. Mc suppression? I think so. There was about 5-8 wallets that
> received the drop from pumpfun bonding curve. All have been selling
> carefully"
> — [29 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-29.md) (❤×6)

The conviction is genuine and personal, not just performed for the room:

> "Fuck it, I'm not selling, you lot are the top G's fr"
> — [28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md) (❤×4)

> "My last thoughts for tn. If tate is the real ticket it gives NO ONE the
> incentive to use Tates new ai creator cause investors have lost and the
> detectives (us) have lost, it's a complete shambles. Tateai is the ticker
> and we do the 200x... Something has to click, we have to win or literally
> everyone loses ✌🏼🫡 singing off. Love"
> — [31 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-31.md) (❤×4, 🔥×1)

## Possible origin of "dancing in the rain"

Wilson uses the phrase "dance in the rain" / "dancing in the rain" from his
very first day — [28 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-28.md):
"Dance in the rain" — and repeats it through the week ("we're just dancing
in the rain," 30 Oct; the slash-tag `/DANCING_IN_THE_RAIN:)`, 31 Oct).
[[widelton]]'s page on this wiki documents "Rain Dance" as his own durable
signature persona, but Widelton's first message lands two days later
([30 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-30.md)), after Wilson had
already been using the phrase. This is circumstantial — nothing in the raw
material has Widelton crediting Wilson or anyone else — but the
chronology is suggestive enough to flag rather than let an existing page's
claim stand unquestioned.

## Burnout and disappearance

Wilson's last dated appearance is explicit about the toll of launch week:

> "Are top holders selling?"
> "Im tired, been awake for 14 days 😂"
> — [9 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-09.md) (👍×1)

No goodbye, no explanation — just the fatigue note, one more message a few
hours later, and then nothing further in the sampled material.

## The "LIKE WILSON" line — probably not about this Wilson

[[fluxcapacitor]] posted, mid-monologue, on
[2 Jan 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-01/2025-01-02.md):

> "ALL YOU HQVE TO DO IS HOLD ON! LIKE WILSON:)"

This reads at first glance like it could be addressed to the chat member
documented on this page. It almost certainly isn't. By 2 Jan 2025, Wilson
(the person) had been silent for nearly two months — his last message in
the sampled material is 9 Nov 2024, and he never posts again, including
nowhere near this date. Nobody in the surrounding chat log reacts to the
line as if it referenced a known member, either — it passes without
comment amid a stream of other gnomic FluxCapacitor aphorisms about holding
on, patience, and isolation that day ("I WILL VANISH LIKE A WARM BREEZE,"
"PATIENCE IS NOT THE ABILITY TO WAIT, BUT THE ABILITY TO KEEP A GOOD
ATTITUDE WHILE WAITING"). Read alongside that theme, "LIKE WILSON" much
more plausibly references **Wilson the volleyball from *Cast Away*** — the
pop-culture shorthand for holding on through isolation — than this chat
member. Neither reading is confirmed anywhere in the raw material; this
page states the more plausible one plainly rather than leaving the
ambiguity unresolved.
