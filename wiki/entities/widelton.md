# Widelton

Unusually for this chat's high-volume launch-era posters, Widelton doesn't
burn out. His first message lands
[30 Oct 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-10/2024-10-30.md), two days into the
chat's life, and his most recent message in this archive is
[13 Jul 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-07/2026-07-13.md) — a continuous presence
of roughly two years and nine months, spread far more evenly across that
span than a launch-era figure like [[g_always_lucky]]. Over that time he
moves through three distinct phases: chaotic launch-night spammer,
self-mythologizing regular, and eventually community moderator and unofficial
fan-site builder.

## Launch era: slash-command spam and the ":)" heartbeat

Widelton's earliest messages are bursts of the same all-caps slash-tag
posted five to ten times in a row — `/TATEAIisREADINGourCHAT`,
`/REAL_NIGGER_TATE`, `/SHAKEOUT_INBOUND`, `/BIG_DICK_VS_ERECTILE_DYSFUNCTION`
— a format he returns to for years (`/HELL_FUCKING_YEAH`,
`/RAIN_DANCE_`). He's also one of the heaviest individual users of the
bare-`:)` pattern this whole chat is known for (see [[smiley-heartbeat]]),
posting it alone, stacked, or as a running bit — "Music On = :) / Music off
= :(" — from his first week onward. A large share of his launch-week volume
is combative back-and-forth with `@TateAI_Bot`, goading it with philosophical
challenge questions and pasting long AI-generated interpretive paragraphs
that read cryptic Terminal tweets as proof the coin is real.

## Self-mythology: the Ghost, the Void, the Rain Dance

Within his first two weeks Widelton settles into a recurring persona —
"a Ghost, moving across the shadows," filling "the Empty Void" — built out of
a small set of phrases he repeats for the rest of his time in the chat, most
durably **"Rain Dance"**:

> "$TATEAI at 80mc i will tattoo TRUMP on me (letters). Screenshot, save it,
> Remind me and Ill make it done. Just because im a Delulu. Widelton Word."
> — [4 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-04.md)

> "Rain Dance is a way of life"
> — [14 Nov 2024](../../raw/logs/2024-11/2024-11-14.md)

No follow-up confirming or denying the tattoo turns up anywhere later in the
material sampled for this page. He also occasionally posts in Hebrew during
this period, suggesting an Israeli background, and — a genuinely
uncomfortable, load-bearing feature of his voice worth flagging plainly
rather than sanitizing away — addresses fellow members and the bot alike
with "nigger"/"niggers" as a casual in-group term from his first week
through the 2026 material, functionally interchangeable with "G" or "bro" in
his usage.

## Becoming a moderator (Jan 2025 onward)

> "Whos the G that made me a admin"
> — [5 Jan 2025](../../raw/logs/2025-01/2025-01-05.md)

From this point on Widelton is functionally chat staff: running `/report`
and `/ban`, and — for well over a year afterward — posting a standing
`/ca` (contract address) plus sticker combo as a greeting ritual for
newcomers, alongside a near-daily "Gm." He continues asking for expanded
admin permissions as late as
[15 May 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-05/2026-05-15.md) ("as an admin can you
give me more permissions?"), suggesting partial rather than full admin
rights throughout.

## Builder era: the TATE AI Terminal fan site (2026)

Starting around April 2026, Widelton's energy shifts into building an
unofficial, ARG-styled community website — first at `widelton.github.io`,
later migrated to `terminal-tate.github.io` — an interactive "terminal"
with typed commands (`status`, `price`, `signal`, `scan`), a hidden
`dossier` page, and lore copy written in-character as a surveillance AI. He
describes coding it "with Claude and GPT," recruits other members to help
push it on X, and frames the proceeds of a companion token ($TCHIP) as
charitable ("going to tates team so they can feed childrens"):

> "We are here over two years G."
> — [28 Apr 2026](../../raw/logs/2026-04/2026-04-28.md)

This is the clearest throughline across his whole run: whatever the phase —
spammer, admin, builder — Widelton is consistently one of the people still
actually *doing* something for the room, long after the launch-week crowd
thinned out.
